# Closest to Tier 4 — MA Phone Policy Progress Report

*Snapshot of the no-mistakes MA Power Map tracker. Generated 2026-07-10.*


This report ranks Massachusetts public school districts by their distance to the Childhood Index gold standard for phone-free schools — **bell-to-bell + inaccessible storage + K-12 + all PEDs + narrow exceptions (IEP/504 + documented medical only).** Each district below has been verified through district records + news + social media triangulation.


For each district, this report names the **exact change** that would clear the tier-4 bar — what to ask the school committee to revise.

## Districts at Tier 4 (gold standard, today)

### Pioneer Valley School District

**Summary:** SC unanimously adopted 2024-06-06, effective Aug 2024. Yondr magnetic pouches (INACCESSIBLE STORAGE — meets Childhood Index gold standard) across ALL 3 PVRSD schools: PVRS (7-12), Bernardston Elementary, Northfield Elementary. K-12 within district footprint. Covers phones, smartwatches, earbuds, tablets — broader than phones. Featured in MA AG's Office statewide toolkit. SC Chair: 'accommodations can be made if absolutely necessary' (per Childhood Index: sup discretion is fine).


**chIdx concerns still flagged (loopholes, but not disqualifying):**
- 'Accommodations can be made if absolutely necessary' — broad rather than specifying IEP/504/documented-medical (but per Childhood Index, sup discretion is fine)

**Sources verified:** 5 (district records + news + social media triangulation)

### Mount Greylock School District

**Summary:** Policy JT (SC adopted 2024-07-22, effective Sept 2024): student-owned PEDs (phones, watches, tablets, earbuds, gaming, smart glasses) powered off and stored bell-to-bell across all 3 schools (LES, WES PreK-6, MGRS 7-12). Storage: lockers at MGRS/LES, front office at WES. Tiered consequences 1st-3rd offense + ISS. Principal discretion for medical or educational purposes (per Childhood Index spec, principal/sup discretion is fine).


**chIdx concerns still flagged (loopholes, but not disqualifying):**
- accessible_storage_lockers_at_MGRS_LES (Childhood Index: lockers are explicitly NOT inaccessible enough; spec wants pouches/cabinets)
- educational_use_exception (Policy JT §4 includes 'educational purposes' carve-out — flagged as weakening but not disqualifying)

**Sources verified:** 6 (district records + news + social media triangulation)

## Notable Tier 2 districts

These districts have policies on the books but lack inaccessible-storage hardware. The Childhood Index spec considers off-and-away (pockets, backpacks, lockers) materially weaker than removal-from-possession (Yondr, locked cabinets).


### 1. Lee School District
*Enrollment ~648 · scope: 6-12 · enforcement: off-and-away*

**Why it's tier 2:**
- pockets_with_off_is_NOT_hardware (Eagle editorial explicitly contrasts Lee with hardware-using McCann/Lenox)
- extraordinary_emergency_carve_out (admin-discretion exception beyond medical/IEP/504)

**Existing strengths:**
- Bell-to-bell 7:58a-2:30p time window codified
- Smartwatches/AirPods/headphones included in scope
- Berkshire Eagle editorial praised consistency

## Methodology

- All entries red-team verified across **district records (school committee minutes, BoardDocs, district handbooks)**, **news coverage (local papers, regional outlets, statewide press)**, and **social media (Facebook, X, Reddit, student newspapers)**.
- Tier system anchored to the **Childhood Index / Distraction-Free Schools Policy Project Model Bill** spec.
- Federal medical / IEP / 504 accommodations are allowed at every tier; superintendent / principal discretion is allowed at tier 4; emergency-use and educational-use exceptions are flagged as loopholes but not auto-disqualifying.
- The **Statewide mandate (S.2561)** takes effect September 1, 2026 — all MA public school districts will be required to implement bell-to-bell phone-free policies. This report tracks who's ahead of that mandate and who has the strongest implementation today.
